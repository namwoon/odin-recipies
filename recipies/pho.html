Pho Recipie!

Ingredients

    2 large onions, split in half
    1 large hand ginger, split in half lengthwise
    3 pounds beef shin, with meat attached
    2 pounds oxtail, cut into 1/2 to 1-inch thick slices
    1 pound boneless beef chuck
    1 pound beef brisket
    3 whole star anise pods
    1 cinnamon stick
    1 teaspoon fennel seeds
    4 cloves
    1 teaspoon fennel seeds
    4 cloves
    1 teaspoon coriander seeds
    1/4 cup fish sauce, plus more to taste
    2 tablespoons sugar (preferably yellow rock sugar)
    Kosher salt

To Serve:

    6 to 8 servings pho noodles
    1 pound beef flank steak, sliced thinly against the grain
    2 to 3 cups mixed herbs (cilantro, basil, and mint)
    2 to 3 cups trimmed bean sprouts
    1/2 cup sliced scallions
    Thinly sliced Thai chiles
    2 limes, each cut into 4 wedges
    Hoisin sauce and Sriracha

Directions

    Place a wire or cooling rack or gril grate over flame and place onions and ginger on top to cook. Turn occasionally until deeply black on all sides, about 10 minutes total.
    Meanwhile, combine beef shins, oxtail, chuck, and brisket in large stockpot. Cover with cool water and bring to boil over high heat. Boil 15 minutes and dump water and meat into sink. When cool, rinseparts under cool running tap water and carefully scrub debris off bones and out of cracks in meat. Return to pot and cover in cool water.
    Add charred vegetables, anise, cinnamon, fennel cloves, coriander, fish sauce , sugar, and 1 tablespoon salt. Bring water to boil over high heat, reduce to bare simmer, and cook skimming occasionally until meat is tender but nto fallling apart (about 1 1/2 hours). Transfer chuck and brisket to small bowl and cover with cold water. Fridge until ready to serve.
    Continue simmering for 4 hours, topping with water as needed. Strain through fine-mesh strainer. Discard bones and aromatics. You should end up with 4 quarts broth (dilute or reduce as necessary to reach 4 quarts). Keep hot.
    Skim fat off surface of broth and discard. Season broth to taste with fish sauce, salt, and/or sugar. Slice cooked beef into thin slices or rough chunks.
    Prepare pho noodles according to package directions. Place re-hydrated noodles in individual bowls. Pur hot broth over noodles. Serve immediately, allowing guests to top with cooked meat and slices of raw flank steak, herbs, aromatics, ilme, and sauce as they wish.
